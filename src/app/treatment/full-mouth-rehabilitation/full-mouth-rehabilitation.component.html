Full Mouth rehabilitation

Full mouth rehabilitation refers to rebuilding and/or replacing all of the teeth in a patient’s mouth. Full mouth rehabilitations combine esthetics with the science of restorative dentistry to improve the health, function, and beauty of the mouth.

Who Requires a Full Mouth rehabilitation?

There are a number of people who have dental problems throughout their mouths that must be treated comprehensively with a vision of a final result that improves both function and esthetics. These patients may exhibit multiple missing teeth, numerous teeth with large fillings that are failing or exhibiting decay, cracked or broken teeth, or badly worn teeth due to bruxism (teeth grinding) or other habits.There is also a group of patients who were born with conditions such as Ectodermal Dysplasia, Ameliogenesis, or Dentinogenisis Imperfecta that will need extensive restoration of their teeth. These patients may be candidates for a full mouth rehabilitation.
